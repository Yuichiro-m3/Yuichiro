Company overview

Why GUIDE MATCH exists.

GUIDE MATCH connects international visitors with Japanese university students who can guide local, flexible Tokyo experiences.

Company overview.

GUIDE MATCH is a student guide matching service for visitors who want to understand Tokyo through everyday local culture. Instead of presenting only fixed sightseeing routes, the service matches a visitor's area, interests, tour length, and language needs with Japanese university students who know the city from a younger, local point of view.

The service is designed for flexible city walks around Tokyo, Yokohama, Kamakura, and nearby neighborhoods. A visitor can ask for food, anime, temples, shopping, nightlife, photo walks, campus life, or simple train support, then choose a student guide whose profile feels right.

Service type Student guide matching
Main area Tokyo and nearby cities
Main users Visitors and university students

Founding story.

The idea started from a simple travel problem: many visitors know where they want to go, but not how to experience those places naturally. Trains, restaurant ordering, local manners, language barriers, and small cultural questions can make a first day in Tokyo feel harder than it needs to be.

GUIDE MATCH was created to make that first connection easier. A Japanese university student can guide a visitor through stations, local shops, food spots, campus neighborhoods, and casual conversations that a guidebook cannot easily provide.
