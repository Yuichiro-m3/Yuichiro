What is GUIDE MATCH?

GUIDE MATCH is a simple way for international visitors to meet Japanese university students who can guide them through Tokyo with local knowledge, language support, and a student perspective.

How visitors and students connect

Requests become guided local experiences.

A visitor shares their travel area, interests, tour length, and message. GUIDE MATCH compares that request with student guide profiles, then recommends students whose local areas, interests, languages, reviews, and availability fit the trip.

  • 1

    Visitor request Travelers choose areas like Shibuya, Asakusa, Yokohama, or Kamakura and add interests such as food, anime, culture, or photography.

  • 2

    Smart guide match The service ranks Japanese university students by area fit, shared interests, language support, reviews, and tour length.

  • 3

    Meet and explore The selected student confirms the route, meets the visitor at a public station, and guides them through local Tokyo.

Local, casual, and built for real conversations.

This is not a formal bus tour. It is a matching service concept for international visitors who want to spend a few hours with a Japanese university student and experience Tokyo like a local friend is showing them around.

How the matching works.

Keep the experience simple for both sides: visitors explain what they want, students confirm availability, and both can chat before meeting in a public station area.

01

Visitor sends a request

Area, date, tour length, interests, language level, and travel notes are collected before introducing student guides.

02

Students receive matches

Guides are ranked by interests, neighborhood knowledge, language, and availability. The visitor can choose who feels right.

03

Meet safely in Tokyo

Meet at a major station, confirm the route, enjoy the tour, and leave reviews so future matches become more accurate.
